# A20 — Minimal Symmetry-Breaking Information

## Proposition 1: growth history no-go

Let `R` be a product order generated by latent coordinates `(X_i,Y_i)`.
Let `A_i` be independent continuous birth times and let `pi` be their rank
ordering. The full sequential history is

`R_k = R restricted to {pi(1),...,pi(k)}`.

For strictly increasing `T_X,T_Y`, the final relation is unchanged and the
birth ranking is unchanged. Hence every `R_k` is unchanged. Observing the
entire filtration gives no information about marginal transformations that
was absent from the final order.

## Proposition 2: invariant and independent marks no-go

Any mark constructed from the order, coordinate ranks, birth ranks, and
transform-invariant randomness has the same coupled value under the
transformation. Marks independent of the coordinates with a model-invariant
law also leave the marked-order distribution unchanged.

## Proposition 3: non-invariant calibrated marks can break the degeneracy

Suppose a mark has a known conditional law `K(dm|x,y)` that is not invariant
under the monotone transformation. If the induced mark distributions differ,
the marked models are statistically distinguishable.

For the witness

`M_i | X_i ~ Poisson(kappa X_i)`,

`E[M_i]=kappa E[X_i]`. The uniform, power, and Beta marginal models used here
have different expectations. Therefore the sample mean of the marks
converges to a different constant in each model, yielding a consistent
distinguishing rule.

## Limitation

The Poisson mark is a mathematical witness only. The audit does not claim
that nature supplies this mark, nor that it is a physical clock, matter field,
or fundamental rate.
